Privacy Policy

Private Female Biomarker. Last updated: April 25, 2026.

Private Female Biomarker is a personal lab record-keeping app for iPhone. This policy explains what the app stores, what may leave your device, and what choices you control.

Data stored on your device

The app stores your saved lab reports, marker readings, lab names, dates, notes, source copies, app preferences, and reminder settings on your device using Apple platform storage. The app does not require an account.

Camera, photos, files, and notifications

If you grant access, the app uses the camera to photograph lab panels, the photo library to import existing panel images, and file access to import PDFs. If you enable reminders, the app schedules local notifications for future lab draw reminders.

On-device processing

Lab report text extraction uses Apple's on-device Vision framework where supported. Your source files, OCR text, notes, and lab names are kept on your iPhone by default unless you choose to export them.

Optional AI explanations

If you tap to create an AI explanation, the app sends only structured marker data needed for that request, such as marker names, values, units, draw date, source type, and reference range context, to the app's proxy service. The app does not send your source file, OCR text, notes, or lab name for this feature.

Exports and sharing

If you export a JSON file, clinician summary PDF, chart, or source copy, that file leaves the app only through the share or export action you choose.

Purchases

Subscriptions and purchase management are handled by Apple's App Store. Private Female Biomarker does not receive your full payment card details.

Tracking and advertising

The app does not track you across other companies' apps or websites and does not use third-party advertising SDKs.

Children

The app is intended for adults. Do not use the app if you are under 18.

Medical disclaimer

Private Female Biomarker is a personal record-keeping tool. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified clinician about your lab results.
